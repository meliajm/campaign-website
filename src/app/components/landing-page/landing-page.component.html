What is an Alderman

An Alderman is a section of the city council which aid in management of the city budget, contracts, taxes, business permits, and financial loans.
The business contracts are "won" through a bid process in which the Alderman reviews.

History

Elizabeth has lived in Berwyn since 1999 where she has raised three beautiful children: Lizette, Xavier, and LizaMarie. She is proud of the rich culture and diversity that Berwyn has to offer and is running for office to ensure the same variety in our government. Elizabeth wants Berwyn to be a place where we can learn from each other and look out for our neighbors. She believes that, through service and modeling, we can collaborate, engage, inspire, learn, accept, and help our fellow citizens. Elizabeth has a long history of serving the community. She has served as a teaching assistant at Heritage Middle School, an administrative assistant at LaVergne Educational Center, and a second-grade bilingual teacher at Pershing School in Berwyn. Following those accomplishments, she set her sights on building our future and was elected to the District 100 Board of Education in 2015 and is currently serving a second term. Elizabeth also actively participates in a variety of professional and community organizations. She is now an educator in DuPage County. Elizabeth’s professional, educational, and proven community service background makes her a vital asset to the city council. She continues to accomplish and serve though the following achievements: Education: Bachelors Degree from Illinois State University in Elementary Education with an emphasis on Multi-cultural Education. Masters degree from Concordia University in School Leadership.
