About Elizabeth O. Jiménez

Elizabeth O. Jiménez is a proud Mexican Womxn. She has lived in Berwyn for the past 22 years. She is a single mother of 3 beautiful children, all of whom have attended Piper Elementary School and Heritage Middle school in District 100. She has had a passion for education and teaching for the past 20 years. Scroll down to get to know Elizabeth more...

1969

Elizabeth was born in Michoacán, Mexico

1988


Elizabeth and her family became legal residents of the United States of America thourgh the Immigration Reform and Control Act (IRCA) passed in November 6, 1986 (Amnesty of 1986). She grew up in the Pilsen Neighborhood of Chicago where she attented Benito Juarez High School.

1998

Elizabeth became a Citizen of the United States of America.

2001

She started working as a Teaching Assistant at Heritage Middle School (Berwyn School District 100). Later she became the School District receptionist and Administrative Assistant to the Director of Building and Grounds. It was during that time that her drive for becoming an educator grew.

2007

She received a degree in higher education from Illinois State University with a Multicultural/Bilingual Education teaching certificate and became the first in her family to graduate from higher education. Then she started teaching 2nd grade at Pershing Elementary School.

2012

Elizabeth earned a School Leadership certificate from Concordia University. (masters?)

2014-2015

Elizabeth left Pershing Elementary in the hopes of beginning her journey into a new role as a potential school board member. In 2015, becoming the first Latina and minority ever to be seated on the Berwyn South School District 100 board

After the 2016 National election

She co-founded an organization called Berwyn Comunidad en Acción, (Berwyn Community Action-BCA) and has worked on making Berwyn a Welcoming City by approving a Safe School resolution in Berwyn South School District 100 that protects undocumented families, students, and staff. The BCA has gained support from ARISE Chicago, BCA organized so that Berwyn’s city council did not opt out of a minimum wage that had been approved in Cook County.

2017

Elizabeth was granted the Faithful Leadership Award from ARISE Chicago.

2017- 2019

Elizabeth served as the first Latina Vice-President on the board and was reelected in 2019.

2014-PRESENT

Elizabeth currently teaches 3rd grade English Language at Maercker Elementary School in DuPage County. As an educator, she has been actively involved with the National Education Association (NEA) and the Illinois Education Association (IEA) and is currently an elected Board of Director to the IEA.
